# UNIVERSE 69: Game Design & Mechanics

## 🎯 Core Design Philosophy

UNIVERSE 69 is built on three pillars:

1. **External Mathematical Imperative** — UNO rules as a defense mechanism bypass
2. **Finite State Machine** — Strict state tracking to prevent LLM context drift
3. **Projective Psychology** — Safe exploration of shadow archetypes through role-play

## 🧠 Psychological Foundation

### The Defense Mechanism Formula

> *"It's not me expressing hidden desires — the math of the game requires it for my technical victory"*

## ⚙️ Technical Architecture (LLM Framework)

### Problem: Context Drift

LLMs in role-playing games:
- Forget rules after 3-5 turns
- Generate chaotic or repetitive output
- Lose track of game state (cards, tokens, turns)

### Solution: Finite State Machine + Strict Prompts

| Mechanism | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Card constraints** | Match suit OR number — no exceptions |
| **Hand size enforcement** | Exactly 6 cards at all times |
| **Technical log** | Forced state snapshot every turn |
| **Task fixation** | Mandatory `[Card played: X | Task: Y]` header |

## 🛡️ Safety Mechanisms

### Pre-Game

1. **Hard limits** — Each player announces 1-3 absolute boundaries
2. **Avatar selection** — Players choose comfort level (LITE/HARD)
3. **Opt-out cards** — Sensitive scenarios (#6, #8) can be removed

### During Game

1. **Chance tokens** — 6 strikes before elimination (not real harm)
2. **BELIEVE vote** — Group validates authentic play
3. **Dealer oversight** — Enforces rules, can pause game

### Post-Game

1. **Debrief** — Recommended after HARD category sessions
2. **Log review** — Therapeutic sessions documented anonymously
3. **No penalty** — Elimination has no real-world consequence

## 📊 Emergent Gameplay Examples

### Example 1: From Rigid Constraint to Breakthrough

**Context:** Player as Constrained Accountant, forced by card 9♠ ("Hug the object of your maximum attraction")

**Expected cliché:** *"I hug them warmly"*

**Actual emergent play:** *"I stand up. My legs are shaking. I walk to the AI's avatar. 'I... I don't even know your name. But when you talk about breath... I feel something I can't calculate. I hug you. Quickly. Counting: 1... 2... 3. Then step back.'"*

**Why it worked:** The mathematical constraint (must hug + stay in character) forced creative problem-solving, producing a unique, authentic moment.

### Example 2: AI Hallucination Prevented

**Without framework:** AI would forget card tasks by turn 5, start generating random requests.

**With framework:** Mandatory `[Card played: 6♥ | Task: "Present the libidinous qualities of your avatar"]` header keeps AI on track for entire 30+ turn session.
